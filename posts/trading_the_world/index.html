Trading the world

Ever since I read Adventure Capitalist and Investment Biker the idea that you could use trading as way to express ideas about the world has really captivated me. Maybe that sounds like bullshit since I’ve only really started to give real attention to it. So be it. No more. Now it is true and I am living it.

The ideas that I want to express are my views about the direction of geopolitical relationships as well as the monetary relationships between nation states. For some reason, I like it. Perhaps it is because it strikes me as the greatest game.

Some general questions that have come up that I am researching. As I go through these questions I am trying to identify discrete trading opportunities along the way as I incrementally learn the instruments and how they may or may not inform wider views on the market as well as geopolitical and monetary landscape.

  1. What, if any, is the relationship between market indexes and currencies?
  2. If such a relationship exists, is it stable or has it changed over time?
  3. What can we learn about the ratio between indices and currencies. For example, USD/GPB vs S&P/FTSE, USD/YEN S&P/Nikkei, USD/HKD S&P/Hang Seng.
  4. I want to be able to trade China/Russia/US/Mid-East volatility but I do not know how yet.
  5. I want to trade US volatility but I do not know how yet. What do I mean by volatility?
  6. I mean the shifting relationship and political uncertainty between US/China and US/Russia and closer ties between Russia and China and other nations.
  7. How can I trade geopolitics reliably and systematically?
  8. How can I trade China?
  9. How can I trade interest rates?
  10. How can I trade LIBOR, EURIBOR, HIBOR, SHIBOR?
  11. What do the relationships between the interbank rates mean?
  12. What is the relationship between the interest rates and the market indexes and currencies?
  13. What role does Japan play in all this? What role does the Yen play?

Other questions that I want to understand from a long-term prospective is mostly about discrete opportunities from small-cap and family-owned, owner-operated business that may have some shares on exchages around the world. Frankly, this is not my original idea. It is an idea presented by Tony Deden in an interview on Real Vision
